Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)

Public Liaison: FOIA-RECORDS-2026

The Department of Prehistoric Wildlife is committed to transparency. Under the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), citizens have the right to access federal agency records, subject to nine specific exemptions.

Before you file:

Many records regarding Specimen Migration and Budget Allocation are already available in our Electronic Reading Room. Please check the public archives before submitting a formal request.

How to Submit a Request

To ensure a timely response, please include the following in your written request:

  • Clear description of the records sought (e.g., "Mist Valley Fence Maintenance Logs, Q3 2025").
  • Your contact information and preferred delivery format (Digital/Hard Copy).
  • A statement regarding your fee category (Commercial, Educational, News Media, or Other).

FOIA Exemptions & Redactions

While the DPW strives for openness, certain information is legally protected from disclosure. Common redactions in DPW documents include:

  • Exemption 1

    National Security: Classified containment protocols that, if released, would enable the intentional release of Alpha-class predators.

  • Exemption 4

    Trade Secrets: Proprietary genetic sequences and cloning methodologies developed in partnership with private bio-tech firms.

  • Exemption 6

    Personal Privacy: Identities of civilians involved in wildlife encounters or trauma triage.

Processing Tiers

Standard Track

20-30 Business Days. For routine logistics and administrative data.

Complex Track

60-90+ Business Days. For genetic research and multi-sector breach reports.

Note: Requests for real-time tracking data of escaped assets will be denied under Exemption 7(F) as such disclosure could reasonably be expected to endanger the life or physical safety of any individual.
