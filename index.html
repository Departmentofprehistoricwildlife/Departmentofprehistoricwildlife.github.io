Coexistence Protocols

Living alongside prehistoric megafauna requires a fundamental shift in daily awareness. The DPW provides the resources needed to keep your community safe.

The Department of Prehistoric Wildlife (DPW) is a global organization within the Jurassic World franchise, established in 1879, that tracks, studies, and manages dinosaur sightings to facilitate human-dinosaur coexistence. It functions as an in-universe agency focused on protection and public safety, similar to wildlife management services.

Ceratopsians

Massive, three-horned herbivores. Generally docile but can become aggressive if they feel cornered or if their young are nearby.Ceratopsia or Ceratopia is a group of herbivorous, beaked dinosaurs that thrived in what are now North America, Asia and Europe, during the Cretaceous Period, although ancestral forms lived earlier, in the Late Jurassic of Asia

Herbivore

Thyreophora

Identifiable by armored plates and spiked tails.Thyreophora, often known simply as armored dinosaurs is a group of armored ornithischian dinosaurs that lived from the Early Jurassic until the end of the Cretaceous. Thyreophorans are characterized by the presence of body armor lined up in longitudinal rows along the body. Avoid the rear-end of the animal at all costs; tail strikes are lethal.

Pack Hunter

Dromaeosaurids

Dromaeosauridae is a family of feathered coelurosaurian theropod dinosaurs. They were generally small to medium-sized feathered carnivores that flourished in the Cretaceous Period.They are Small, fast, and highly intelligent. If you see one, assume there are others you cannot see. Do not break eye contact.
