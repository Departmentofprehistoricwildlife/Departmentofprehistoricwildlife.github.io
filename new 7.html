General Liability Waiver & Release of Claims

Form ID: DPW-REL-2026-TX

WARNING: THIS IS A LEGALLY BINDING DOCUMENT. BY ACCESSING DPW SANCTUARY ADJACENT ZONES OR REMAINING WITHIN A "CODE RED" AREA, YOU ARE WAIVING CERTAIN LEGAL RIGHTS, INCLUDING THE RIGHT TO SUE FOR DAMAGES RELATED TO PREHISTORIC WILDLIFE ENCOUNTERS.

This Release and Waiver of Liability is executed this ____ day of ________________, 20__, by the undersigned (the "Participant/Resident") in favor of the Department of Prehistoric Wildlife (DPW), its directors, officers, employees, and agents.

1. Acknowledgment of Inherent Risk

The Participant acknowledges that prehistoric wildlife (including but not limited to Theropods, Sauropods, and Pterosaurs) are inherently unpredictable, dangerous, and possess physical capabilities far exceeding modern fauna. Risks include, but are not limited to: high-velocity pursuit, crushing injuries, seismic-related property damage, infrasonic disorientation, and predatory stalking.

2. Waiver of Liability for Containment Breach

In consideration for residing in or visiting designated Sanctuary-adjacent lands, the Participant hereby releases and forever discharges the DPW from any and all liability for injury, death, or property damage resulting from a "Code Red" containment failure, regardless of whether said failure resulted from mechanical error, power surge, or animal intelligence.

3. Assumption of Responsibility

The Participant assumes full responsibility for their own safety and the safety of their dependents. This includes the duty to maintain "Hard Shelter" readiness, adhere to blackout protocols during breach events, and comply with all emergency directives issued via the DPW Emergency Portal.

4. Indemnification

The Participant agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the DPW from any claims brought by third parties (including neighbors or guests) resulting from the Participant's failure to secure their premises or follow DPW blackout/silence protocols during an active breach.

5. Emergency Medical Authorization

In the event of a wildlife-related injury where the Participant is incapacitated, the Participant hereby authorizes DPW Rapid Response Teams to administer emergency triage or specialized tranquilization as deemed necessary for public safety.
