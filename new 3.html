Official Safety Directive 402-A

This document outlines the mandatory protocols for all personnel operating within prehistoric wildlife zones. Failure to comply with these directives results in immediate contract termination and forfeiture of life insurance benefits.

Specimen Threat Classifications

Class I: Low Risk

Herbivorous Megafauna

Small to medium grazers. Generally docile unless provoked or in rut.Massive plant-eaters that act as "ecosystem engineers," using immense physical scale and specialized feeding structures to reshape entire landscapes through sheer biomass consumption.

  • Standard field gear
  • 10m minimum distance
Class II: Moderate Risk

Apex Pack Hunters

High intelligence, cooperative hunting behavior.They are social predators that utilize coordinated flanking maneuvers and high-frequency vocalizations to overwhelm much larger targets through tactical attrition. (e.g., Dromaeosauridae)

  • Armed escort required
  • Bio-electric fencing
Class III: Extreme Risk

Giga-Predators

Non-containable during active phases. Sensory-based hunting patterns.They are Solitary titans of the prehistoric food chain that rely on sheer bite force and overwhelming physical scale to dominate territories and neutralize high-tonnage prey.

  • Full seismic monitoring
  • No visual contact permitted

Standard Field Operations

Olfactory Neutralization

Scent-masking spray must be applied every 4 hours. No food items permitted in Class II zones.

Seismic Footwear

Vibration-dampening boots are mandatory to avoid attracting subterranean predators.

Visual Silhouette Management

Personnel must not stand on ridgelines. Break up your outline with localized ghillie tech.

Containment Breach Logic

1

Initiate silent alarm via neuro-link.In the event of a Neuro-Link Silent Alarm, personnel are instructed to find a 'hard-point'—a high-ground location or a reinforced structure—and enter a state of Biometric Suppression.

2

Move to nearest 'Cold Zone' (concrete bunkers, depth > 5m).Reduce heart rate and Minimize movement.

3

Do not attempt to 'heroically' distract the specimen.Stay still do not attempt to communicate via audio.
