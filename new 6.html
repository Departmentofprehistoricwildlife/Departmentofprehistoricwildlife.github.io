Privacy & Data Collection Policy

Effective Date: January 01, 2026 | Document ID: DPW-PP-0922

Notice: This policy covers the collection of data during normal operations and "Code Red" containment events. By residing within a 50-mile radius of a DPW Sanctuary, you acknowledge the data collection methods outlined below.

1. Information We Collect

The Department of Prehistoric Wildlife (DPW) collects information to ensure public safety and specimen containment. This includes:

  • Public Sighting Reports: GPS coordinates, timestamps, and media (photos/videos) submitted via the Emergency Portal.
  • Proximity Data: Automated pings from mobile devices within active "Breach Zones" to assist in evacuation efforts.
  • Biometric Data: Collected only in high-security sanctuary perimeters for authorized personnel.

2. Use of Emergency Data

In the event of a Containment Failure (Level 4 or higher), your data is used for:

  • Directing Rapid Response Teams to sightings.
  • Generating heat maps of specimen movement.
  • Distributing localized emergency alerts via SMS and push notifications.

3. Seismic and Audio Monitoring

The DPW maintains a network of long-range acoustic sensors. These sensors are designed to detect low-frequency prehistoric vocalizations and footfalls. While these sensors may pick up ambient civilian noise, we do not record or store private conversations. Audio data is automatically scrubbed of human frequencies every 24 hours.

4. Information Sharing

We do not sell your data to third-party commercial entities. However, data may be shared with:

  • National Security Agencies: In the event of a multi-state wildlife migration.
  • Local Law Enforcement: For the enforcement of mandatory evacuation orders.
  • Scientific Research Partners: Anonymized movement data used to study de-extinct behavioral patterns.

5. Surveillance and Drones

During active breaches, the DPW utilizes autonomous "Scout Drones." These drones utilize thermal imaging to distinguish between human heat signatures and prehistoric megafauna. Imagery of private property obtained during a chase is considered "Emergency Evidence" and is protected under the Prehistoric Wildlife Act of 2024.

6. Your Rights

Under the DPW Transparency Initiative, citizens have the right to request a summary of the data collected from their devices during a specific emergency window. To request this data, please contact the DPW Records Division via encrypted mail.

Disclaimer: The DPW reserves the right to override standard privacy protocols during a "Level 5 Total Lockdown" to ensure the survival of the local population. Failure to comply with data-driven evacuation orders may result in forfeiture of liability claims.
