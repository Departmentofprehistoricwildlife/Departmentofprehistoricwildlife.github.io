Professional Code of Conduct

Document: DPW-HR-CONDUCT-2026

This document outlines the mandatory ethical and professional standards for all field agents, containment specialists, and research staff employed by the Department of Prehistoric Wildlife. Adherence is a condition of continued employment.

1. Preservation of Human Life

The primary mandate of every DPW Agent is the preservation of civilian life. In any conflict between the safety of a prehistoric asset and human life, human safety must be prioritized without exception.

2. Asset Engagement Protocols

Directive 2.1: Use of Force

Lethal force against prehistoric assets is strictly reserved for "Scenario Omega" events where containment has failed and immediate civilian threat is present. Agents must utilize non-lethal sedative rounds and acoustic deterrents whenever possible.

3. Professional Neutrality

Agents must maintain emotional distance from prehistoric assets. De-extinct fauna are biological constructs, not pets. Naming assets or attempting to establish "bonds" outside of approved behavioral research is grounds for immediate reassignment.

4. Public Communication & Media

Field Agents are strictly prohibited from sharing unauthorized media (photos, videos, or audio recordings) of breaches or sanctuary interiors. All public communication must be routed through the DPW Press Office. Any "leak" of classified T-Rex behavioral data will be prosecuted under the Prehistoric Information Act.

5. Equipment & Tech Discipline

Maintaining the integrity of "Bio-Fencing" and tracking telemetry is the responsibility of all staff. Any detected glitch in thermal sensors or gate mag-locks must be reported to Engineering within 30 seconds of discovery.

6. Ethical Research Boundaries

Research staff must adhere to the Genetic Ethics Accord. The creation of hybrid species or the enhancement of natural predatory traits for military application is strictly forbidden under DPW charter.

Notice of Violation

Violations of this code of conduct during a containment breach may result in immediate suspension of sanctuary access, forfeiture of hazard pay, and/or referral to the Department of Justice.
