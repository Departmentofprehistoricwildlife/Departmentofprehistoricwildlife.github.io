Survival Protocols

Protocol 1: Scent Masking

Most theropods possess olfactory bulbs 40x more sensitive than bloodhounds. Agents must apply DPW pheromone suppressants every 4 hours.Olfactory acuity was highest in tyrannosaurids and dromaeosaurids (raptors), allowing them to detect prey or food from a distance.

Protocol 2: Visual Deterrents

High-intensity magnesium flares are the only proven deterrent for nocturnal hunters. Keep three active flares in your harness.Magnesium flares act as a powerful visual deterrent by producing intense light (up to 12,000 candles) that can startle and repel dinosaurs.

Protocol 3: Night Travel

DO NOT MOVE AT NIGHT. Predators hold a significant sensory advantage in low light. Secure a "nest" at least 15 feet high before sunset.Moving at night in wilderness areas is generally unsafe due to severe limitations in human vision compared to nocturnal predators, who possess superior light-gathering eyes and enhanced hearing.
